Enshittification

B1-B2 · General English · 90 min

Main Objectives

  • Understand the concept of "enshittification" and analyze the lifecycle of digital platforms as they prioritize profit over user experience;
  • Expand vocabulary related to digital platforms and practice using verbs with the prefix "en-";
  • Critically evaluate the ethical implications of data privacy, targeted advertising, and profit-driven degradation across various modern industries.

About This Lesson

In this lesson, students begin by matching definitions to a wide array of digital platforms (e.g., microblogging sites, content management systems, photo-sharing apps) and discussing their personal usage. They then expand their lexical range by exploring words with the prefix "en-" (e.g., enrage, enlighten, entangle, envision). The core introduces the term "enshittification" — the three-stage lifecycle of platforms: starting with a great product to attract users, degrading the experience to benefit advertisers, and finally extracting all remaining value through subscriptions. Students then analyze how tech giants like Facebook and Instagram actually make money, watching a video about targeted advertising, data collection, and the Cambridge Analytica scandal. Finally, students explore how "enshittification" extends beyond tech into airlines (hidden fees, shrinking legroom), video games (microtransactions), smartphones (planned obsolescence), urban housing, and journalism (clickbait), brainstorming other areas where systems are degrading.
