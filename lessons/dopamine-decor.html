Dopamine Decor

B1-B2 · General English · 90 min

Main Objectives

  • Explore the relationship between brain chemicals (such as dopamine, serotonin, and oxytocin) and environmental stimuli;
  • Learn and apply interior design vocabulary, including patterns, color palettes, and the core principles of "Dopamine Decor";
  • Critically evaluate common interior design mistakes and analyze emerging aesthetic trends to create personalized, mood-boosting living spaces.

About This Lesson

In this lesson, students begin by discussing how they would allocate a hypothetical renovation budget before diving into the science of mood, matching ten key brain chemicals and hormones (e.g., dopamine, cortisol, melatonin, endorphins) to their primary biological roles and natural boosters. They then transition to the concept of "Dopamine Decor," learning how to intentionally use hero colors, bold patterns, and dramatic scale to create joyful spaces that trigger feel-good chemicals. Students personalize this approach by selecting their own seasonal color palettes and preferred art styles. The lesson expands into a broader exploration of modern interior design trends, where learners match emerging styles — such as Biophilic Design, Neurosensory Spaces, Cultural Maximalism, and Dark Dopamine — to visual examples. Finally, students review a guide on the "10 Common Interior Design Mistakes and How to Fix Them," reflecting on their own living spaces and discussing how to balance traditional aesthetic rules with personal happiness.
