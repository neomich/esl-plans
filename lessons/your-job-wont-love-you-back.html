Your Job Won't Love You Back

B2-C1 · Business English · 90 min

Main Objectives

  • Identify and practice functional language for describing job responsibilities, skills, and professional experience;
  • Analyze the shifting dynamics of employer-employee loyalty through listening comprehension and statistical data;
  • Critically evaluate cultural myths about "finding your passion" and the "corporate family" narrative through video analysis and debate.

About This Lesson

In this lesson, students begin by examining famous quotes about "doing what you love" and defining key idioms like to settle and matter of the heart to discuss whether their own jobs are their passion. They then expand their professional vocabulary by categorizing phrases used to describe duties, positions, and skills, before listening to three professionals describe the roles they initially loved. The lesson takes a realistic turn as students listen to the same speakers two years later — now facing redundancy and betrayal — prompting a discussion on the limits of corporate loyalty. Learners analyze statistical data regarding Gen Z's transactional approach to work, side hustles, and dissatisfaction with traditional career paths. Finally, students contrast cultural myths with "brutal truths" by watching videos that challenge the idea of a company as a "second family," exploring the reality that the corporate machine keeps going without the individual. They conclude by examining generational shifts in work ethic and discussing the importance of separating self-worth from professional identity.
