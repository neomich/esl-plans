Teaching B2-C1 Adults Online: 8 Things I've Learned the Hard Way

20 July 2026 · ESL-plans Teacher's Corner

Teaching advanced adults online sounds easy on paper. They already speak English. They know how online meetings work. They're motivated. Well... sometimes. The reality is a little messier.

1. Be platform-flexible

Everyone says Let's use Zoom until Zoom decides not to cooperate. Don't become emotionally attached to one platform. I keep Zoom, Teams and Google Meet ready to go. Technology should disappear into the background.

2. Assume your student is using a phone

A huge number of adults don't sit comfortably behind a large monitor. They're on trains, in hotel rooms, in cafes. Ask yourself: Could someone comfortably use this on a phone?

3. Let visuals do some of the teaching

Adults process visual information incredibly quickly. Sometimes one good image does the job in five seconds. The less cognitive effort spent navigating the worksheet, the more energy students have for the language itself.

4. Expect the lesson to end unexpectedly

Adults have jobs, children, deadlines and unpredictable schedules. Always cover the essentials first. Don't take it personally.

5. Follow up after every lesson

Adults are busy. By tomorrow morning, they've attended three meetings and forgotten half the vocabulary. A quick follow-up message makes a huge difference. Think of it as leaving little breadcrumbs back to English.

6. Be politely persistent

If an adult student forgets a lesson, it usually isn't because they don't care. They're overwhelmed. Don't be afraid to send reminders. You're helping busy people keep a promise they genuinely wanted to keep.

7. Talk about things adults actually care about

By B2 or C1, keeping someone genuinely interested for an hour is the real challenge. People happily forget they're studying English when they're debating whether AI will replace their jobs.

8. Relax a little

Advanced students don't expect perfection. They expect authenticity. The best online classes rarely feel like classes. They feel like two curious people exploring an interesting topic together.
