How to Make Business English Lessons Actually Interesting

20 July 2026 · ESL-plans Teacher's Corner

Let's be honest. Business English doesn't have the best reputation. The problem is that we've somehow convinced ourselves that business people only want to talk about... business. When they log into your lesson, they're looking for a break — not another shift at work.

Business English is still English

I rarely start a lesson by saying Today we're going to practise negotiating. Instead, I might ask: Would you rather have a four-day work week or a 20% pay rise? Now we're talking. Students forget they're in a Business English lesson. The business language appears naturally.

Choose topics that people argue about

Nothing kills a lesson faster than a topic everyone agrees with. Look for ideas that divide opinions — remote work, salary transparency, quiet quitting, AI in the workplace, office friendships.

Use authentic language

Business English textbooks often sound strangely polished. Real workplaces don't. People interrupt. They hesitate. They change direction halfway through a sentence. Students need exposure to the kind of English they'll actually hear on Monday morning.

Let students bring their own experience

One of the biggest advantages of teaching adults is that they've already lived interesting professional lives. Someone has managed a team. Someone has fired an employee. Someone has started a business. Use that. Ask questions. Be curious.

Remember why they're there

Most professionals aren't learning English because they love grammar. They're learning because they want to communicate without feeling anxious. Encourage opinions. Listen more. Build their confidence. The vocabulary and grammar will come naturally when the conversation feels genuine.
